What is TOVO?

TOVO (from Dutch totaalvoetbal) is a methodology rooted in Johan Cruyff's Total Football, developed by Todd Beane. Every exercise is game-based and cognitively loaded — players don't drill in isolation. They train competence, cognition, and character simultaneously through positional play, rondos, and training games that mirror real match problems. Technique is never divorced from decision-making.

90-minute TOVO session arc — rondo → positional → tactical → full game

Phase 1 — Rondo
1

Circle Rondo — 8v3 / 9v3

Perception · Press Resistance · Speed of Play
10 min
Large Circle Rondo Full Squad Splits
Groups: 2 groups of 11
Format: 8 outer · 3 inner
Space: ~18 yd diameter circle
Touch limit: 2 touch

22 players split into two circles. 8–9 players on the perimeter keep the ball away from 3 defenders in the middle. When a defender wins possession or forces the ball out, the player who lost it swaps to the middle. This is the foundational TOVO warm-up — perception, body shape, and rapid decision-making from the first moment of practice.

Diagram · Circle Rondo
TOVO

The rondo is not a warmup drill — it is the core cognitive exercise. Todd Beane calls it a "perception activity." Players must constantly scan to know where all 8 teammates are before the ball arrives. Freeze and ask: "Where is the free man?"

Key Questions to Ask
  • Where is the free man?
  • What is your body shape before receiving?
  • Play away from the pressure — which side?
  • How quickly can we switch the point of attack?
Progressions
  • 1-touch only for outer players
  • Point scored by switching ball across circle through middle (1-touch)
  • 4v2 in pairs: outer player who loses goes in
Phase 2 — Positional Play
2

Positional Play — 8v4+4 (Overload)

Width · Depth · Third-Man Combinations
15 min
Positional Grid with Fixed Wide Players Central Theme
Grid: 40×30 yd
Format: 8 keep + 2 wide targets vs. 4 press
Touches: 3 → 2
Score: Point per 8 consecutive passes

Set a 40×30 grid. Two wide players (one on each touchline) act as neutral targets who always play with the possessing team. 8 outfield players keep the ball against 4. The wide targets are 1-touch and serve as switching options. A point is scored for every 8 consecutive passes, or for successfully switching the ball from one wide player to the other in one combination. This is the Cruyff positional model: create numerical superiority in zones, use width to stretch the press, then exploit space.

Diagram · 8v4 with Wide Neutrals
TOVO

Cruyff's positional play principle: "Create a 3v2 or 2v1 in every zone." The wide neutrals represent the wingers in your real 11v11 shape. Players must learn to use width before it's available in the game.

Coaching Points
  • Occupy all four corners of the grid — spread the press
  • Play away from pressure; use the wide target to switch
  • Third man runs: when two play a combination, a third moves into space
  • Ball side vs. weak side positioning at all times
Progressions
  • Wide targets can now enter the grid to join play (true wingers)
  • Add a directional element: team must play through a central gate to score
  • Add a 5th defender to simulate a mid-block
Phase 3 — Shape & Transition
3

Phase of Play — Build-Out + Transition

Game Model · Collective Organization · Trigger Moments
20 min
9v9 Directional — Build & Press Phase of Play
Format: 9v9 on ¾ field
Field: ~65×55 yd
Goals: Full-size both ends
GK: Included both sides
Theme: Build from back, press trigger on loss

Two teams of 9 (leaving two players as tactical subs/observers). One team sets up in your preferred shape (e.g. 4-3-2) to build from the goalkeeper. The opposing team presses from the front in a coordinated shape. When possession changes, the transition trigger is immediate — ball-winner's team must advance quickly before the shape re-sets; team that lost must reorganize and press within 3 seconds. Coach stops play to organize shape, press triggers, and transition moments. This is the TOVO "training game" phase — context-rich, match-realistic decisions.

Diagram · 9v9 Build-Out Phase of Play
TOVO

TOVO's "Game Model" identifies 6 game phases: organized attack, transition to defense, organized defense, transition to attack, set pieces for and against. This exercise trains the two transition phases intensively — the moments Cruyff called the most important in football.

Coaching Points
  • GK is the first attacker — play through, not over
  • CB splits wide to create width during build
  • On loss: press trigger within 3 seconds — everyone moves
  • First pass after winning possession: forward or wide, never backward
  • Striker: pressing angle cuts off the GK pass
Progressions
  • Add scoring rule: goal scored within 6 seconds of winning ball = worth 2
  • Team must complete 4 passes before shooting
  • Pressing team plays out of a low block instead of high press
Phase 4 — Training Game
4

11v11 Training Game

Application · Freedom · Competitive
25 min
11v11 Full Field — Constrained Game Full Pitch
Field: Full 100×65 yd
Goals: Full size, GKs
Constraint: Goals count double if scored in wide zone
Duration: 2 × 10 min

Full 11v11. One constraint only: a goal scored via a sequence that uses both wide channels before the final pass is worth 2 points. This rewards the positional principles worked on in Phase 2 without over-coaching. Keep stoppages to a maximum of 2 per half — let the game teach. The constraint will naturally push teams to stretch wide and use width, which was the point of the entire session.

Diagram · Full Pitch 11v11
TOVO

The constrained game is how TOVO connects training themes to match reality. The constraint (wide zones = double points) is an invisible coach — it shapes behavior without the coach needing to stop play. Players self-organize toward the session theme.

Coaching Points (max 2 freezes)
  • Are we using both wide channels or collapsing central?
  • Transition speed — can we find a forward pass in 2 seconds?
  • Midfielders: occupy the half-space between lines
  • Shape under pressure: do we compact or stretch?
Remove Constraint If…
  • Teams are forcing wide unnecessarily — just play free
  • Session is going long — remove and let them compete
  • Energy is low — free play resets engagement every time
